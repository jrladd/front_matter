


Noble Sir,
THis Discourse is due to you, upon the account of your favours to Mr. Harrington: And therefore as he was pleas'd to make me the Orator of his thanks to God, so am I also the Messenger of his thanks to You. I am a great Ad∣mirer of your Honours unshaken Loy∣alty, and other your great Virtues; and I am as devout a Beedsman for your lasting Prosperity. That there∣fore Heaven may bless all that Wis∣dom,
 Conduct and Integrity, which shines so gloriously in you above most others, is the daily Orison of,
Noble Sir,
Leigh in Essex, Nov. 21. 1682.
