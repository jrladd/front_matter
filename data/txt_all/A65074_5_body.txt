This Sermon was preacht upon one of the dayes of your solemn approach and drawing nigh to God. It was no season to present you with fancies dressed in cobweb lawn, or thin notions cloathed with ayr. The Popish fasts did but change solid meat into sweet meats. The subject of the Sermon is cleansing and purifying; which as it was necessary by the law, before the unclean person might draw nigh to God, so it is required of you; If thou prepare thine heart, and stretch out thy hands towards him; If iniquity be in thy hand, put it farre away: for then shalt thou lift up thy face without spot, Iob. 11. 13, 14, 15. It is a liberty which Ministers claim by vertue of their commission, to speake, exhort, and rebuke with all authority, Tit. 2. 15. You that
