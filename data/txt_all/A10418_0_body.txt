



WHo bites his lip, when his folly is bitten, hath either enuy in his heart, or suspicion in his head: whome I esteeme as  Ians, do  hedge-bred Serpents, tread on their backs, and pull out their stings. But of your worthy selfe, (whose censure vertue will singularly sentence) I craue an eye cast to these rough cast Satyres, which are not absurded (though some∣what rustically mustred) whose neuer forgotten fauours I fling vp, not downe, in cha∣lenge of all witlesse vnarmed heedpces.
