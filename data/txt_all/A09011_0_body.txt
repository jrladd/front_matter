



HAving by long paines and endeavours, composed this Manlike Worke of Herbes and Plants, Most graci∣ous Soveraigne (as I formerly did a Feminine of Flow∣ers, and presented it to the Queenes most excellent Majesty) I could doe no lesse then submissively lay it at your Majesties feet, to be approved or condemned, and if thought fit and worthy a publique passage, to offer it on the Altar of your Majestyes many favours to me, to be commanded as well as com∣mended unto all for their owne good. For as your Majesty is Summus Pater patriae, the chiefe of your people under God, that not onely provi∣deth for their soules health, that they may have the pure Word of God, whereby to live ever, wherein we justly claime the prerogative above a∣ny Nation under Heaven, and most devoutly praise God for the same, and desire religiously to live thereunder: but many wayes also for their bodily estates, by good and wholesome Lawes, that every one may live obediently and peaceably under their owne Vine and Figtree, and by protection, &c. And I doubt not of your Majesties further care of their bodies health, that such Workes as deliver approved remedyes may be divulged, whereby they may both cure and prevent their diseases.
